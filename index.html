Multi-Modal Intelligence

We are a joint research team led by investigators at the University of Pennsylvania (UPenn) and the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign (UIUC), focused on building next-generation multimodal intelligence systems for real-world scene understanding and geospatial reasoning. Our vision is to move beyond single-modality by tightly integrating audio, vision, and RF sensing with physics-informed machine learning. We study how signal propagation, 3D geometry, and learned representations can be combined to solve inverse problems and infer where events occur and how scenes can be reconstructed. The three themes below highlight our prior and ongoing work in acoustic and audio intelligence, computer vision and machine learning, and localization systems.

Research Topics

Three core directions that connect our joint UPenn-UIUC research agenda.

Acoustic and Audio Intelligence

We model acoustic fields and room impulse response, and study inverse acoustics to recover scene structure and source characteristics from real-world recordings. By combining physical propagation priors with learning-based inference, we make audio a reliable modality for robust environmental reasoning and location inference.

More Details ↗

Computer Vision and Machine Learning

We develop multimodal learning and inverse rendering methods that infer geometry, semantics, and uncertainty from sparse or noisy signals. This line of work connects visual observations, generative priors, and cross-modal supervision to improve scene understanding with complementary capabilities across multiple modalities.

More Details ↗

Localization Technologies and Systems

We build end-to-end localization systems using RF, acoustic, and visual sensing. Our research emphasizes system-level design that tightly integrates sensing hardware, signal processing, and machine learning. We develop practical systems capable of robust localization in complex and dynamic environments.

Acoustic and Audio Intelligence

Recent project highlights spanning acoustic field reconstruction, generative audio editing, and inverse acoustic reasoning.

AVR: Acoustic Volume Rendering and Neural Impulse Response Fields

NeurIPS 2024 (Spotlight)
AVR models a neural impulse-response field for indoor scenes and renders room acoustics at unseen source-listener pairs. The model combines physical wave propagation priors with learning-based interpolation to improve realism under sparse measurement settings. This project demonstrates how audio can recover scene-dependent structure and support robust multimodal reasoning.
Project Page ↗

Versa: Resounding Acoustic Fields with Reciprocity

NeurIPS 2025
Versa introduces reciprocity constraints to learn acoustics from limited indoor recordings while preserving physical consistency. By coupling source-listener role symmetry with neural acoustic fields, it improves generalization across novel placements. This is a core example of physics-informed machine learning for acoustic intelligence.
Project Page ↗

Building Audio-Visual Digital Twins with Smartphones

MobiSys 2026
This project reconstructs editable digital twins from commodity smartphone captures by jointly estimating scene geometry and acoustic behavior. It supports updates to materials and layout while preserving plausible acoustics through differentiable rendering. The result is a practical path toward scalable multimodal environment modeling.
Paper ↗

SmartDJ: Declarative Audio Editing with Audio Language Model

ICLR 2026
SmartDJ studies language-guided audio manipulation where users specify semantic edits and the system executes them on complex tracks. The approach links natural language intent to controllable audio generation pipelines and improves editing efficiency for non-expert workflows. It contributes a multimodal interface between language and structured audio transformations.
Project Page ↗

ArrayDPS: Unsupervised Blind Source Separation with a Diffusion Prior

ICML 2025
ArrayDPS addresses multi-source audio separation by combining microphone-array observations with diffusion priors in an unsupervised setup. It tackles noisy and reverberant mixtures where ground-truth labels are unavailable and classical beamforming is brittle. The project highlights robust audio intelligence under realistic sensing constraints.
Paper PDF↗

Inverse Acoustics for Scene Decomposition

Work in Progress
This line of work decomposes wet recordings into dry source signals and room impulse responses to infer latent scene factors. The system estimates direct source content and propagation effects jointly, supporting downstream localization and scene retrieval. It serves as a bridge from acoustic inverse problems to practical multimodal geospatial reasoning.

Computer Vision and Machine Learning

Recent project highlights on generative modeling, inverse solvers, and multimodal representation learning for scene understanding.

Next-Scale Autoregressive Models for Text-to-Motion Generation

CVPR 2026
This project develops autoregressive generative models for controllable text-to-motion synthesis at higher fidelity and temporal consistency. It improves the mapping from linguistic intent to structured motion trajectories and supports richer downstream visual reasoning. The work demonstrates how sequence modeling and generative priors can improve multimodal perception tasks.

CO3: Contrasting Concepts Compose Better in Diffusion Models

ICLR 2026
CO3 investigates concept composition in diffusion models by introducing contrastive mechanisms that improve compositional faithfulness. It targets failures where multiple semantic constraints compete during generation and improves controllability. The project is central to robust multimodal generation and inverse reasoning.
Paper PDF↗

Zero-shot Human Pose Estimation Using Diffusion-based Inverse Solvers

ICLR 2026
This project frames pose estimation as an inverse problem solved with diffusion priors, enabling zero-shot adaptation without retraining per domain. The method improves robustness when supervision is limited or sensing conditions shift. It directly connects probabilistic generation with geometric estimation in computer vision pipelines.
Project Page ↗

Contrastive Diffusion Guidance for Spatial Inverse Problems

ICLR 2026
This work studies contrastive guidance for diffusion-based solvers in spatial inverse tasks, where ambiguity and noise make direct optimization unstable. It uses structured priors to regularize reconstruction and improve convergence toward physically plausible solutions. The approach strengthens general-purpose multimodal inverse modeling.
Project Page ↗

Can NeRFs "See" without Cameras?

NeurIPS 2025
Can NeRFs "See" without Cameras reconstructs 3D structure from non-visual measurements and extends neural scene representations beyond camera-centric pipelines. The method shows that geometry can be inferred from alternative sensing signals while preserving rich spatial detail. It is a key project at the intersection of CV, ML, and multimodal sensing.
Project Page ↗

RIR-to-Depth: Hearing Room Geometry

Work in Progress
RIR-to-Depth explores an inverse-learning pipeline that predicts panoramic depth structure from room impulse responses. It leverages generative priors to resolve ambiguity and produce physically consistent scene hypotheses. This project provides a CV/ML pathway for translating acoustic signatures into visual geometric representations.

Localization Technologies and Systems

Recent project highlights on end-to-end localization pipelines using RF, acoustic, and multimodal sensing in realistic environments.

RF-Based 3D SLAM Rivaling Vision Approaches

MobiCom 2025
This project develops RF-based 3D SLAM with accuracy competitive with camera-based methods while operating in challenging visibility conditions. It integrates mapping, trajectory estimation, and geometric consistency checks under strong multipath effects. The work demonstrates deployable localization performance beyond imagery-only sensing.
Project Page ↗

Surface Characterization with mmWave Signals

MobiSys 2026
Surface Characterization with mmWave Signals estimates material and geometric properties of indoor surfaces using radar reflections. The extracted surface cues improve downstream localization and scene interpretation in spaces where visual metadata is incomplete. This contributes robust physical context for multimodal positioning systems.

Motion Capture with Millimeter-Wave Tags

SenSys 2026
This project studies tagged mmWave motion capture for reliable tracking in cluttered indoor spaces and under weak lighting. It fuses radio measurements with learned priors to stabilize trajectory and pose estimates over time. The resulting system expands the design space for localization-centric sensing deployments.

Non-Line-of-Sight 3D Reconstruction with Radar

NeurIPS 2025
Non-Line-of-Sight 3D Reconstruction with Radar infers hidden geometry from indirect reflections and solves challenging occluded-scene localization settings. The model combines radar physics with learned reconstruction objectives to recover usable spatial structure. This is an important capability for indoor positioning without direct line of sight.
Project Page ↗

PanoRadar: Enabling Visual Recognition at Radio Frequency

MobiCom 2024
PanoRadar builds panoramic scene recognition from RF signals and demonstrates strong perception performance under occlusion and adverse visual conditions. The approach produces localization-relevant spatial context when cameras are unreliable or unavailable. It is a key system-level example of multimodal localization technologies.
Project Page ↗

Voice Localization using Nearby Wall Reflections

MobiCom 2020
Voice Localization uses direct-path and reflected speech signals to estimate source position with commodity microphones in indoor environments. The system is designed to remain robust under room reverberation and practical deployment constraints. It is a foundational localization project connecting acoustic propagation and real-world sensing.

Multi-Modal Intelligence

We are a joint research initiative led by investigators at the University of Pennsylvania and the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign, focused on building next-generation multimodal intelligence systems for real-world scene understanding and geospatial reasoning. Our vision is to move beyond single-modality pipelines by tightly integrating audio, vision, and RF sensing with physics-informed machine learning. Across this portfolio, we study how signal propagation, 3D geometry, and learned representations can be combined to infer where events occur, what environments look like, and how scenes can be reconstructed from incomplete observations. The three themes below highlight our prior and ongoing work in acoustic and audio intelligence, computer vision and machine learning, and localization systems.

1) Acoustic and Audio Intelligence

Projects on acoustic field modeling, inverse acoustics, and signal decomposition for robust scene understanding from audio.

AVR: Acoustic Volume Rendering for Neural Acoustic Field

NeurIPS’24 (Spotlight)
AVR models an impulse response field using a neural representation inspired by volume rendering. It synthesizes RIRs at novel listener/source poses and supports efficient training and rendering, bridging real measurements and realistic simulation.
Project Page

Versa: Reciprocity for Acoustic Field Learning

NeurIPS’25
Leveraging acoustic reciprocity as supervision, this line of work enforces physical consistency when learning acoustic fields from sparse measurements. Swapping emitter ↔ listener roles improves generalization for predicting RIRs at new source positions.
Project Page

Audio-Visual Twin Reconstruction

To appear at Mobisys'26
AVTwin reconstructs editable audio-visual digital twins from commodity smartphones by combining mobile RIR capture with visual-assisted acoustic field modeling. The result supports material and geometry edits with acoustics updated via differentiable acoustic rendering.
Paper (arXiv)

2) Computer Vision and Machine Learning

Multimodal and inverse-learning projects connecting acoustic cues with visual and geometric scene representations.

Can we “hear” the shape of a room?

Work in progress
We explore RIR-to-Depth estimation, a new inverse acoustic rendering problem that aims to infer the panoramic depth map of an indoor environment using only recorded RIR. To tackle this ambiguity, we investigate generative models that learn the distribution of plausible room geometries. The model learns to reason about uncertainty and generate geometrically consistent layouts that explain the observed echoes. This have lots of potential in the acoustic localization since we can estimate the rough indoor environment from just acoustic signatures and match the potential room layout to the floor plan database for localization.

Inverse Acoustics

Work in progress
In the inverse acoustics, we are trying to decompose the acoustic scene into the basic components including the audio content (dry sound) and acoustic effects. For example, a common voice recording can contains two speakers voice. And the recordings (wet sound) are also affected by the room acoustics. Our goal is to effectively decompose both the two dry speech sound sources and the room acoustic effects (RIR) from the mixed recording. This can be very useful for acoustic localization since we can estimate the room acoustic effects from the recording and match it to the floor plan database for localization. 3) Localization Systems

Selected prior systems in acoustic and RF localization from Mingmin Zhao and Romit Roy Choudhury.

Voice Localization using Nearby Wall Reflections

MobiCom 2020
A practical acoustic indoor localization system that estimates source position by exploiting direct-path and reflected voice signals. This project demonstrates robust localization from commodity microphone recordings in real indoor environments.
Paper PDF

Can NeRFs See without Cameras?

NeurIPS 2025
This work reconstructs indoor 3D structure from wireless reflections and demonstrates localization-relevant geometric inference without optical cameras, advancing multimodal mapping and positioning.
Paper (arXiv)

PanoRadar: Visual Recognition with RF Signals

MobiCom 2024
PanoRadar builds panoramic scene understanding from RF sensing, illustrating the group's experience in non-visual localization and perception where standard camera signals are limited.
